## Refined Thesis (load-bearing)

**Twisted Pin is a bar-program-led venue.** The website sells the bar, not the bowling alley.

Two cornerstone differentiators carry equal weight:

1. **A craft cocktail program built by *America's Top Mixologist*** (Food Network) — Brian Van Flandern, who set the program up as a consultant and is back to retrain the team summer 2026. He is not staff, not an owner, not a daily presence; the credential is the lever, the relationship is the truth. Per Se / Thomas Keller / Michelin three-star framing is **fully retired** — does not appear anywhere on the website, including long-form body copy (see 2026-05-03 decisions log).
2. **The 28-tap self-serve beer & wine wall** — unique in our immediate market.

The bowling experience exists, but it isn't the lead. **When bowling shows up on the homepage, it's the VIP suite specifically** — that's the actual differentiator vs Bowlero / Lucky Strike, not "we have lanes." Traditional lanes get acknowledged lower on the page.

**Reference frame:** Flight Club Chicago and similar dark experiential venues — they lead with food/beverage, the activity is implied, customers figure it out fast. We do the same.

**Adults-first, family-welcome — segmented by hour, not by audience.** The venue self-segments by hour: Saturday at 6pm is family-dinner-and-bowling; Saturday at 11pm is friends-and-cocktails. **Same room, different night.** Adult-quality is the *lever* (the reason to choose us over Chuck E. Cheese / Bowlero / D&B); family-welcome is the *permission slip* (the reason it's OK to bring the kids). Don't lead with family copy ("family-friendly fun" — banned); don't write copy that implies kids aren't welcome either. The adult is always the chooser; the choice is easier when the parent knows the kid will ask to come back. Female-led decision-making, mixed-gender photography, real groups. The 1am close is a positioning asset, not just an ops fact — it signals adult time exists, which is what gives family time permission to exist without contradiction. (Earlier *"adults-first, group-friendly, family-welcome in that order"* formulation read as a grudging hierarchy; reframed 2026-05-11 to co-equal time-segmented modes.)

## Working Preferences

- **Mobile-first.** 90% of traffic is mobile. Design and test mobile before desktop. Don't build desktop and squish.
- **Mobile and desktop hero are separate decisions.** Vertical video hero on mobile; hero photograph on desktop. Never letterbox or stretch the vertical video to fit desktop.
- **Page-speed targets are non-negotiable.** Mobile LCP < 2.5s, performance score 85+. See `seo.md` for the full table. Every other SEO win is wasted if speed isn't there.
- **Lock visual decisions before structure work.** Color, type, hero composition decided first. Then sections. Then components.
- **Push the edge, don't play it safe.** I'd rather see you push the attitude on copy and I dial back than see safe-corporate defaults. Confident and playful with attitude is the register. Just not gross.
- **Don't bake new design language into everything before it's approved.** If you propose a new pattern (e.g., the scribble art treatment), mock it up on a placeholder page first.
- **No homepage decoration that fights the moody thesis.** Arcade and LED-video-wall photography do not appear on the homepage. They live on `/arcade/` and `/bowling/` respectively.
- **Specifics beat adjectives.** *"17 traditional lanes + a 6-lane VIP suite"* beats *"upscale bowling experience."* Use real numbers in copy and alt text.
- **Brian Van Flandern in cocktail/bar contexts only.** Don't force the name into food, bowling, arcade, or generic copy.
- **"Private" is banned in copy.** No surface (page, ad, bio, schema, alt text) describes the VIP suite — or anything else at the venue — as "private." It's *semi-private*. Use "yours for the night," "reserved for your group," "set apart from the main floor," or "semi-private suite" if access nuance matters. The full rule is in `voice.md` (Words to Avoid). User has flagged this multiple times — don't break it.
- **"Cheap / discount / value / deals / budget-friendly" is banned in copy.** Premium positioning — never write price-discount language anywhere on the site, including the `/coupon` page (renamed from `/free-10` 2026-05-04). Frame offers without leaning into discount vocabulary. Full rule in `voice.md` Words to Avoid.
- **Family-welcome reality vs adults-first positioning.** The venue IS family-friendly — most attendees are families. But copy leads adults-first because the adult is the one who needs convincing (the inverse of Chuck E. Cheese: there it's family-friendly and adults don't want to be there, so they market families; here it's family-friendly and adults DO want to be there, so we market adults). Don't deny families; don't market with "family-friendly fun" / "fun for the whole family" / "family-friendly" as the lead noun. Family-friendliness is a fact, not a brand angle. **Two-mode framing (2026-05-11):** the venue self-segments by hour — family-dinner at 6, friends-and-cocktails at 11, same room, different night. The 1am close is the structural signal that adult time exists, which gives family time permission to exist without contradiction. See `voice.md` "Adults-first, family-welcome — segmented by hour, not by audience" section.

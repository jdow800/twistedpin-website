# Twisted Pin — Visual Direction

The web look-and-feel reference. Pairs with `brand-guidelines.md` (logo, lockups, typography names) and `voice.md` (copy, tone). This file overrides the original *"vivid celebration of the night and neon lights"* brief — see "What Changed" below.

---

## What Changed (Deprecated Brief)

The original Twisted Pin brand guidelines describe a *"vivid celebration of the night and neon lights"* — leaning Midnight Indigo + Glow in the Dark + neon fuchsia/orange/yellow accents. **That direction is deprecated for the website.**

We've drifted toward something **moodier and more neutral**: dark/saturated backgrounds, warm wood and copper accents, photo-led layouts, bold display typography, generous negative space. Closer to a contemporary cocktail bar / chophouse than a neon arcade. The accent colors from the brand palette still appear, but as *strikes* — not the foundation.

Why: the audience (Experience-Seeking Socializer, planning the night) reads neon-and-glow as "Bowlero." Reading moody-and-confident is what reads as premium and adult-first.

The brand-guidelines color hex values themselves still hold (Midnight Indigo, Nautical Blue, etc.). What's changed is **how much real estate they get** and **how often the accents appear**.

---

## Visual Thesis (one sentence)

**Bold display typography, dark/moody backgrounds with selective warm accents, photo-led, confident negative space, minimal ornamental decoration.**

Adjective shorthand: *moody, editorial, photo-forward, type-led, confident.*

---

## Positioning Thesis (what the site is selling)

**Twisted Pin is a bar-program-led venue.** Two cornerstone differentiators carry equal weight on the homepage:

1. **A craft cocktail program built by Brian Van Flandern** — Michelin three-star mixologist, former head mixologist at Thomas Keller's Per Se. One of the highest-level cocktail programs in the area.
2. **The 28-tap self-serve beer & wine wall** — unique in our immediate market.

The bowling experience exists, but it isn't the lead. **When bowling shows up on the homepage, it's the VIP suite specifically** — that's the actual differentiator vs Bowlero / Lucky Strike, not "we have lanes." Traditional lanes get acknowledged lower on the page, not in the hero.

**Reference frame:** Flight Club Chicago and similar dark experiential venues — they lead with food/beverage, the activity is implied, customers figure it out fast. We do the same.

What this changes for visual decisions:
- The hero shouldn't be cocktail-forward only. It should signal **"premium bar program"** — could be cocktail, could be the wine/tap wall, could be both across mobile/desktop.
- The cocktail editorial photos and the wine/tap wall photo carry equal hero weight. Don't default to one.
- The VIP suite is the bowling shot when bowling appears. The traditional-lanes wide shots stay below the fold or on `/bowling/`.
- Arcade and LED video-wall photography stay off the homepage entirely.

## Visual Front-Runners (in order)

The reference sites the site should feel close to:

1. **Jut-So** — overall favorite, longtime reference. Look at: saturated single-color backgrounds, oversized type as the design itself, illustrative one-off hero element, generous negative space, no ornamental clutter. *Twisted Pin reading*: same confidence in single-color fields, but our color is dark instead of purple, and our hero element is a real photo or video instead of an illustration.
2. **Pints & Paddle** — for menu / section treatment. Look at: verb-driven all-caps grid (PLAY / POUR / DINE), navy + warm yellow, photo-meets-type tile pattern.
3. **Swingers** — for menu / section treatment. Look at: dark-navy + gold, refined type, vertical rhythm, premium-feel divider lines.
4. **Foodtruck Club** — secondary. Look at: chunky black display headlines on a warm cream ground, full-bleed lifestyle photo as evidence, sticker-style accents used sparingly.

What we are not chasing: Flowfest's cream / sticker maximalism, Truus's editorial italic-serif mix, FC88's retro-neon-on-black. Useful as adjacent references but not load-bearing.

## Color — Adjusted Usage

The hex values from `brand-guidelines.md` stand. The *role and proportion* of each is what's been re-balanced.

### Foundation (most of every page)

| Role | Color | Hex | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary background | **Midnight Indigo** | `#200F53` | Deepened/desaturated toward near-black in practice. Used full-bleed. |
| Secondary background | **Near-black** | `#0E0A1F`–`#1A1530` | Mood floor. Use behind hero, photography sections. |
| Surface / card | **Slightly lifted indigo** | `#2A1B66` | For elevation only. |
| Body text on dark | **Warm off-white** | `#F4EFE3` | Not pure white. Reads warmer, more editorial. |

### Photography accents (frequent)

The room itself is teal/copper/wood. Lean into that — pull these from the photos rather than overlaying brand color.

- **Warm wood** (the floors, tables, cocktail bar)
- **Copper / rose-gold** (the BEER HELPS sign, copper mugs, copper shakers)
- **Teal** (the booth upholstery and accent walls — close to Nautical Blue but a touch more green)

### Brand accents (used as strikes — not defaults)

| Color | Hex | Use case |
|---|---|---|
| **Glow in the Dark** | `#4EECC4` | Hover states, link underlines, single-element accents (a CTA arrow, a badge, a number). Rare on the homepage. |
| **Tango IPA** | `#FF8000` | Cocktail/bar moments, badges. Sparingly. |
| **Flirty Fuchsia** | `#D45D92` | Date-night / event-promo accents. Rare. |
| **Lemon Twist** | `#FFE236` | Avoid as a default; reserve for arcade/redemption-prize sections only. |
| **Celestial Blue** | `#468CC8` | Bowling-section header accent. Used as small fill, not full-bleed. |

> Rule of thumb for accents: **one accent color per section, max two per page.** If everything's an accent, nothing is.

## Typography — Web Application

Brand fonts stand: **Proxima Nova** for headlines and UI, **Yorkten Slab** for body. Web-licensed via Adobe Fonts. Rajdhani / Gala / Ohm / Luxie / Thirsty Script remain "icing only" — use deliberately, not as default.

### Hierarchy on the homepage

- **Display** (hero / section headlines): Proxima Nova **Black** or Extra Condensed Black, all caps where it suits, generous letter-spacing tightening at large sizes. Treat as a design element — large enough to feel typographic, not just a label.
- **Sub-display**: Proxima Nova Bold Italic or Yorkten Slab Bold for tag-line moments and stickers.
- **Body**: Yorkten Slab Regular at 16–18px on mobile, 18–20px on desktop. Slab gives editorial weight without feeling stuffy. Line-height generous (1.55+).
- **Eyebrow / metadata** (small caps, dates, captions): Proxima Nova Bold, all caps, tracked, 11–13px.
- **CTA buttons**: Proxima Nova Bold or Black, all caps, tracked.

### Mood
Pints & Paddle and Swingers both treat type as architecture — sized big enough to *be* the layout, not just sit on it. We do the same. Headlines should feel like signage.

### Avoid
- Mixed-display-font collages (a la Flowfest / Truus). Looks busy in our brand context.
- Decorative scripts as default headline. Thirsty Script is for one-off date-night moments, not standard H1s.
- Italic body copy at length.

## Composition Principles

1. **Negative space is part of the design.** Let dark fields breathe — Jut-So's confidence comes from *not* filling the canvas. Per page: pick the one image or one block of type that's the moment. Everything else can be quieter.
2. **Type-as-design, not type-as-label.** Headline scales should feel typographic at every breakpoint. On mobile, headline can run 56–80px and dominate the first viewport.
3. **One accent color per section.** Pull color from the photo, then add at most one brand-accent stroke (a hover, a divider, a small icon).
4. **No ornamental decoration.** Brand guidelines mention the "Formation" arrows and "the Pin" mark — use them functionally (as a divider, as a directional cue, as a section marker), not as space-filler. If you can remove a decorative element and the page reads stronger, remove it.
5. **Photography over illustration.** We have a real room and real people. Lean on that. Illustration is a last resort.
6. **Mobile-first layout.** Most visitors are on a phone. Design the mobile composition first, then expand. Don't build desktop and squish.

## Hero — Mobile vs Desktop (Two Separate Decisions)

### Mobile hero
Vertical 9:16 video, autoplay-muted-loop. See `mobile-hero-recommendation` section in the next handoff for the specific clip recommendation between *Bank Vs Stories* and *Best Things To Order*.

### Desktop hero
Hero photograph (single image or subtle Ken-Burns), **not the vertical video letterboxed or stretched**. The cocktail-pour or cocktail-with-bowling-lane-bokeh shot is the strongest candidate from the current library. Headline overlay anchored to lower-left or center; CTA below the fold of the hero region but above the page fold.

The reason the two heroes are separate decisions: the vertical video shows energy/motion in a tall frame; the desktop hero shows composition/atmosphere in a wide one. They are different jobs. Letterboxed video on desktop reads as a placeholder and undermines the polish of everything else.

## What "On-Brand" Looks Like (visual gut-check)

A page is on-brand when:

- A passerby reading the homepage at arm's length thinks *cocktail bar*, *chophouse*, or *upscale lounge* — not *bowling alley*
- The first viewport on mobile has one bold headline, one beautiful photo or video, one clear CTA, and a lot of dark negative space
- Brand-accent colors appear once or twice per scroll, not in every component
- The single most expensive-feeling photograph is the cocktail or event shot — the bowling-lane shots support, they don't lead
- The headline is large enough to feel typographic, not text-as-label

A page is **off-brand** when:

- Neon arcade photography or electric LED-wall lights dominate the first viewport
- Multiple brand accent colors appear in the same component
- "Family-friendly fun" is implied by lighting or staging
- The Pin mark or Formation arrows are used as ornament rather than function
- Decorative scripts (Thirsty Script) are used outside of a one-off seasonal moment
- The page tries to be both moody-cocktail-bar AND neon-arcade-fun in the same scroll
